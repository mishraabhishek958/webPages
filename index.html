Here's a timeline of Mr. Tendulkar's life:


  • Sachin Ramesh Tendulkar
  • Born : 24 April 1973 (age 44)[1] Bombay (now Mumbai), Maharashtra.
  • National side : India (1989–2013)
  • The highest run scorer of all time in International cricket.
  • Tendulkar made his Test debut on 15 November 1989 against Pakistan in Karachi at the age of sixteen.
  • He is the only player to have scored one hundred international centuries.
  • He is the first batsman to score a double century in a One Day International.
  • He is the holder of the record for the most number of runs in both ODI and Test cricket.
  • Tendulkar was a part of the Indian team that won the 2011 World Cup, his first win in six World Cup appearances for India.
  • Tendulkar received the Arjuna Award in 1994 for his outstanding sporting achievement, the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna award in 1997.
  • He recieved Padma Shri and Padma Vibhushan awards in 1999 and 2008.
  • He is the youngest recipient to date and the first ever sportsperson to receive the Bharat Ratna Award,highest civilian award of India.
  • On 24 May 1995, Tendulkar married Anjali Mehta (b. 1967), a paediatrician, whom he had first met in 1990.They have a daughter and a son, Arjun, whose skills in cricket as a teenager have been receiving attention.
  • In 2012, Tendulkar was nominated to the Rajya Sabha, the upper house of the Parliament of India.
  • He was also the first sportsperson and the first person without an aviation background to be awarded the honorary rank of group captain by the Indian Air Force
  • In December 2012, Tendulkar announced his retirement from ODIs.He retired from Twenty20 cricket in October 2013.
  • He has not been a part of any illegal activity on the cricket field.
  • Tendulkar played 664 international cricket matches in total, scoring 34,357 runs.
